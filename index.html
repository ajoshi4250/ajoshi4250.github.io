About

I am a freshman Computer Science student with a French minor at the University of Maryland. I am also a part of the Public Leadership Scholars program. Hailing from Ellicott City, Maryland, I am a graduate of Howard High School’s class of 2020. In high school, I was Co-Captain of the Debate Team and an active member of the Math and French National Honor Societies. At UMD, I am involved with the Maryland Parliamentary Debate Society and the Libraries’ Student Advisory Group. I have a strong research background and am a published author who plans to join a Ph.D. program once I graduate.

I joined the Public Leadership Scholars program because I want to gain a strong foundation in public policy. One of my research area of interest is the intersection of public policy and computer science. I want to gain a better understanding of this intersection and how to close the gap between technology and its regulations from my time in this Scholars Program. I also want to gain a better understanding of foreign policy and global issues which is why I chose this program and my minor in French. I hope to have a successful academic career and be able to explore truly intersectional research areas in my time at UMD and with the Public Leadership Scholars Program.

Selected Work

I have been able to apply what I have learned through the Scholars program this semester to different writing assignments. I have added a selected few I believe show my progress this semsester.

Oprah Winfrey Policy Brief

From PLCY201, Public Leaders and Active Citizens, I have chosen my policy brief on the Oprah Winfrey Leadership Academy for Girls. I enjoyed writing this paper and I believe that given the constraints on outside sources, I was able to write it well. I did not get a perfect score on this paper (only a 90/100), but it demonstrates my ability in this class. I honestly found this to be the most interesting topic for the policy briefs and I also thought it sparked interesting conversation for the in-class debate.

Living Democracy Reflection

From my Scholars colloquium, I picked my reflection on Living Democracy by Lappé and DuBois. I found this reading quite interesting and I feel that I have reflected that in my response. Although this assignment has not been graded, I think it is a good representation of my skills in class. I also believe that this reflection is on the other end of the spectrum from my policy brief, yet they both showcase my writing style and thought process in a flattering way. I personally quite enjoyed writing this reflection and hope it is enjoyable to read.

Myself As A Leader (Final Paper for CPPL)

I picked the Myself As A Leader paper that I wrote as my final paper for CPPL 100 because I felt it discussed a topic I am interested in and a modern-day leader I admire. I think that this paper also shows my future goals and what I want to be as a leader well. This also ties in concepts from class I found to be the most interesting. The flexibility with this paper allowed me to write something which portrays me. I appreciated the thought this provoked and felt that this paper’s prompt is one that should be used in other classes as well.

Other Work at UMD

While Scholars has been an important part of my UMD experience, I have also been grateful to pursue a Computer Science major and a French minor, both of which have been important in my academic path.

I am currently enrolled in CMSC131 learning basic object-oriented programming in Java. Next semester I plan to take CMSC132 and CMSC250, object-oriented programming II and discrete structures, respectively. I have my class this semester to be enjoyable and have successfully completed a variety of projects, quizzes, and exams, maintaining an A+ in the class. I look forward to my upcoming years as a Computer Science major.

This semester I am also enrolled in FREN250, cultural and textual analysis, and plan to take FREN301, composition and style, next semester. I have been learning French since the seventh grade and will graduate with a French minor. I have been enjoying my time and doing well in class. I love learning languages and am teaching myself a few. During my time at UMD, I hope to improve my French and take courses in Japanese and German.
